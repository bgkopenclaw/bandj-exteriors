# B and J Exteriors

## Register
brand

## Product purpose
A marketing site for B and J Exteriors, a brand-new power washing business in Bergen County, NJ, owned and operated by three local high school students: Ben, Justin, and Brady. The site exists to convert local homeowners into phone calls and on-site estimates. Most visits come from mobile, often from a Google or word-of-mouth search after the visitor has decided they need someone but don't know who. The page has roughly one fold to feel "real" and one minute to surface a phone number.

The team being high schoolers is part of the offer, not a disclaimer: the site should signal that these are local, serious, hardworking kids who care about doing each job the right way and being good members of their own community. The visual brand still reads grown-up and trade-confident — no kid-stylings — but copy doesn't pretend to be a decade-old business.

## Users
**Bergen County homeowners** (35–70). Often dual-income, suburban single-family, judging contractors quickly. Want their decks, patios, driveways, and pool surrounds brought back without anyone wrecking the landscaping. Want to feel that the person showing up will respect their property and be reachable if there's a problem.

This audience wants one proof: this is a real local business, run by humans whose names and numbers are on the page.

## Brand voice
Three concrete words: **wet**, **material**, **quiet-confident**.
- *Wet* — water is the medium and the work. The brand doesn't sell pressure-washing as a service; it sells what surfaces look like after water did its job.
- *Material* — concrete, pavers, wood, brick, painted metal. The site should feel like surfaces, not screens.
- *Quiet-confident* — the work speaks. No exclamation points, no "elevate your property," no "premium" adjectives. Tradespeople who don't oversell.

## Anti-references
- **SaaS-template "trust + CTA" pages.** Navy + accent-blue gradients, Inter, 3-equal-card service rows, rounded icon tiles above headings, gradient CTA bands, generic trust strips.
- **Stock-clean home-services pages** with a stock-photo hero of a guy holding a wand. Identical-card grids of services. Five-star testimonial cards. Generic "Why choose us" with checkmark lists.
- **Editorial-typographic magazine pages** (Fraunces italic + monorule + drop caps). Wrong register for a trade business.
- **Hand-painted folk-shop kitsch** that pretends the business is older than it is.
- "Local · Reliable · Insured" pill grids.

## Strategic principles
1. **Mobile is the surface.** Most leads tap a phone number within 30 seconds. The phone number is the design's primary CTA, not a styled button next to one.
2. **Three names, three numbers.** Ben, Justin, Brady. Not "our team." The site visibly contains three contactable humans.
3. **Imagery is the design.** Photos of water hitting a deck, concrete coming clean, a truck with a hose reel — these carry the brand. Type is in service to image.
4. **Single dominant idea per fold.** Don't compress five things into a hero. Long scroll, deliberate pacing.
5. **Bergen County is part of the brand.** Service area names appear as type, not as filler chips.
